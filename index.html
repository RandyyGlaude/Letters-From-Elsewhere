Quiet Mornings in the Valley

The trail was empty and the air was still. A thin ribbon of fog sat between the ridges, softening every edge. I brewed tea on a flat rock and watched the light move inch by inch across the valley floor.

Gear was minimal: a small camera, a notebook, and a scarf for the cold start. Sometimes that’s all you need.

Notes: Shot on a 35mm prime. Edited lightly for contrast. Route from the east car park to the saddle and back.

On Packing Light

I’ve been trimming the day pack: water, phone, tiny tripod, and a compact camera. Most days I don’t miss the heavyweight lenses.

The trade-off is simple: less strain, more wandering. If a shot needs a bigger setup, it can wait for another day.
